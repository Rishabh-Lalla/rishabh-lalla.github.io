About

I am currently pursuing a PhD in the Computer Vision Department at the Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI), where I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Muhammad Haris Khan. Earlier, I served as a Research Associate with Lab1055 at the Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad, working under the mentorship of Prof. Vineeth N. Balasubramanian. My research interests include Domain Generalization, Domain Adaptation, Continual Learning, Vision–Language models, and Diffusion models.

Research

My work aims to make computer vision systems robust across changing domains, labels and tasks. I explore algorithms that can adapt without access to target data labels (domain adaptation), generalize to unseen domains (domain generalization) and handle partial or noisy labels. My research also touches on test‑time adaptation, object detection, vision‑language models and continual learning.

Current Projects

  • Source‑free Domain Adaptive Object Detection: Identifying and mitigating noise at both the pseudo‑label and feature levels. Leveraging ideas from noisy‑label learning and diffusion guidance to improve detection performance.
  • Multi‑Target Unsupervised Domain Adaptation: Extending our ECCV 2024 work by proposing domain‑wise candidate pruning to handle large candidate sets. Combining partial‑label and noisy‑label learning techniques to improve performance across multiple target domains.
  • Unsupervised Domain Generalization: Developing methods that generalize to unseen domains without labelled target data.
  • Future directions: Preparing a survey on domain generalization and exploring rotated object detection in vision‑language settings.
